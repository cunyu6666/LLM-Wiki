# High-Performance Teams in the Age of AI: Learnings from Lovable

## 中文翻译

> **AI 时代的高绩效团队：** 来自 Lovable 的经验

### 像创始人一样思考

一家公司能遇到的最好事情，就是人们不再像员工那样行事。创造最大价值的人不会等许可，不会问"这归谁管"。他们看到问题，揽过来，直接干。**你没法指派主人翁意识，你只能雇到那些天生就有的人。**

### 雇态度，而非技能

我们当然看技能。但我们已经意识到，单凭技能几乎无法预测成功。真正能脱颖而出的人，靠的是好奇心、韧性和愿意学习一切岗位所需之物的态度。在 AI 时代，这一点比以往任何时候都重要。

### 保持真正的好奇心

"用 AI"和"痴迷 AI"之间有天壤之别。那些创造最大杠杆的人，总是在不断探索、测试没人让他们测试的东西、追随可能永远没有结果的想法。大多数确实没有结果。但少数几个，改变一切。**好奇心的复利效应，比几乎任何事物都快。**

### 让资深的人重新深入一线

我见过最有意思的事情之一，就是经验丰富的领导者重新变回一线建设者。AI 极大地放大了个人贡献者的杠杆率。**一个高度资深的运营者加上 AI，是现代公司中最强大的组合之一。**

### 在自我膨胀杀死势头之前干掉它

我从没见过自我让一家公司变快。但我见过它拖慢无数家。人们保护自己的点子、争夺所有权、为认可而非结果做优化。最快的团队出奇地不在乎功劳归谁。他们在乎的是——什么有效。

### 超出预期地在意

我合作过的顶尖人才都有一个共性。他们在意得更多。更在意产品，更在意客户，更在意细节。他们注意到别人忽略的东西，修复没人让他们修的东西。这很难衡量，也无法教会。但一旦你见过，你就会明白，多少进步来自那些真正在意的人。

### 先发，再迭代

我学会了一件事：相信用户，胜过相信观点。一周的内部辩论，学到的东西远不如一天的真实反馈。最高绩效的团队不执着于发布前做到完美。他们执着的是尽可能快地学习。**发布，就是他们学习的方式。**

> **High-Performance Teams in the Age of AI:** Learnings from Lovable

### Think like a founder

The best thing that can happen to a company is when people stop behaving like employees. The people who create the most impact don't wait for permission. They don't ask whose responsibility something is. They see a problem, claim it, and run with it. **You can't assign ownership. You can only hire people who naturally take it.**

### Hire for attitude, not skills

We absolutely hire for skills. But we've learned that skills alone rarely predict success. The people who thrive are the ones with curiosity, grit, and the willingness to learn whatever the job requires. In the age of AI, that matters more than ever.

### Stay genuinely curious

There's a huge difference between using AI and being obsessed with it. The people who create the most leverage are constantly exploring, testing things nobody asked them to test, and following ideas that may never lead anywhere. Most don't. A few change everything. **Curiosity compounds faster than almost anything else.**

### Let senior people go deep again

One of the most interesting things I've seen is experienced leaders becoming builders again. AI has dramatically increased the leverage of individual contributors. **A highly experienced operator with AI is one of the most powerful combinations in modern companies.**

### Kill ego before it kills momentum

I've never seen ego make a company faster. I've seen it slow down plenty of them. People protecting ideas, fighting over ownership, optimizing for recognition instead of outcomes. The fastest teams care surprisingly little about who gets credit. They care about what works.

### Care more than expected

The highest performers I've worked with all share one trait. They care more. More about the product. More about the customer. More about the details. They notice things others ignore and fix things nobody asked them to fix. It's hard to measure and impossible to teach. Once you've seen it, you realize how much progress comes from people who genuinely care.

### Ship, then improve

I've learned to trust users more than opinions. A week of internal debate rarely teaches as much as a day of real-world feedback. The highest-performing teams aren't obsessed with getting things perfect before launch. They're obsessed with learning as quickly as possible. **Shipping is simply how they learn.**
